Personal Injury Cases

Truck vs Work at Construction Site

Result: Settled for $4.2 million, Fresno County Superior Court

A 42 years old foreman was working for the general contractor at a work site on Highway 99. He was supervising a crew that was performing shoulder work that involving applying asphalt. A truck containing asphalt material was backing towards a spreading machine when it hit the plaintiff, knocked him to the ground and the rear tire ran over his hip and thigh. Plaintiff sustained substantial injuries, including fractured femur that needed a surgical rod and nerve injuries to his leg.

Auto vs Pedestrian

Result: Verdict of $1.3 million, Fresno County Superior Court

Represented a 22 year old woman who was a student at Fresno State University. She was walking on a sidewalk near the library when a person backing his pick-up truck jumped the curb and hit knocked her to the ground. She sustained a fractured hip and had orthopedic problems with her legs.

Truck vs Auto Accident

Result: Verdict of $1.3 million, San Luis Obispo Superior Court

Represented the defendant trucking company in an action that arose from a rear-end collision with stopped vehicle. 30 year old woman sustained a fractured femur that needed to be repaired with a rod and fracture of her lumbar spine. Plaintiff’s attorney requested damages in excess of $2 million. Liability was admitted and there was a three week trial to determine amount of recoverable damages.

Train vs Truck Accident

Result: Case settled for minimal amount.

Defended a trucking company in a lawsuit filed by the engineer of a train that hit a truck stopped across the railroad tracks. We investigated the accident and conducted discovery that established the truck became stuck because the road to the tracks had an excessive slope in violation of PUC regulations.

Slip and Fall Accident

Result: After extensive discovery the case settled on favorable terms.

Represented a retail store in a slip and fall accident. Plaintiff alleged she slipped on a magazine subscription card, which caused an injury to her shoulder. Plaintiff had shoulder surgery and her recovery was complicated by pulmonary embolism and cardiac arrest. Plaintiff claimed cognitive deficits due to hypoxia and that she required nursing home.

Construction Site Accident

Result: A court trial was held and judgment entered for general contractor that reimbursed the settlement paid and recovered attorneys fees.

Defended an employee of the general contractor who was driving a golf cart and struck the employee of a subcontractor. Reached a settlement with the employee in order to minimize damages and then proceeded under an indemnity provision against subcontractor for reimbursement of the settlement paid plus attorneys fees.

Products Liability Cases

Defective Automatic Van Door System

Result: Settled for $95,000.

Represented an individual who sustained a torn rotator cuff when an automatic sliding side door on a van closed. A products liability lawsuit was filed against the manufacturer of the vehicle. Through investigation and discovery we established there had been similar incidents and that the handle was designed such that a person’s hand could become trapped.

Conveyor Belt Injury

Result: Summary judgment obtained in the trial court which was affirmed by the Court of Appeals.

Plaintiff filed a lawsuit after this hand became caught in a pinch-point of a conveyor belt. We represented a equipment supplier who sold various parts to a packing house, which had designed and constructed the conveyor belt. Plaintiff alleged the part supplier was liable because it did not advise the plans prepared by the owner could cause hand injuries.

Truck Tailgate

Result: Both cases settled for minimal amounts.

Represented the manufacture of a hydraulic liftgate in two personal injury actions that arose from the individuals sustaining injuries when the slipped from the tailgate. Plaintiffs alleged that the tailgates were defective because too slippery and should have had non-slip paint. Established during discovery that paid had worn off during use of the tailgate by Plaintiffs’ employer.

Box Gluing Machine

Result: Case settled for minimal amount while motion for summary judgment was pending.

Represented the prior owner of a box gluing machine. Plaintiff sustained severe crushing injuries while using the equipment and two fingers were amputated. Plaintiff alleged the prior owner removed safety devises and failed to warn the purchaser of the proper use of the equipement. During discovery established the machine was sold as is and that no safety devices had been removed.
