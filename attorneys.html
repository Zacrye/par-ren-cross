Michael L. Renberg

Michael Renberg is a partner and litigation attorney. He was raised in Fresno, County and graduated from University of California, Los Angeles with a degree in Politic Science. Mr. Renberg then attended the University of San Francisco School of Law and graduated with honors. While at law school he was a member of the Moot Court Board and was selected to be a judicial clerk for David Eagleson of the California Supreme Court.

After graduation Mr. Renberg was a law clerk for a Federal judge, Robert Coyle of the U.S. District Court, Eastern District of California. During the clerkship he handled various aspects of court procedures, including preparing jury instructions, reviewing motions, drafting bench memos and assisting the judge with cases. Since 1994 Mr. Renberg has been a litigation attorney with Parichan, Renberg & Crossman. He handles cases from initial retention, pleadings, and discovery through trial and appeal.

Mr. Renberg focuses his practice primarily in the areas of personal injury, tort defense, products liability, construction defect litigation, and business litigation. In particular, he has extensive expertise in cases involving complex medical causation, such as brain injuries, spinal cord injuries and toxic exposure.

Mr. Renberg also handles mediations for personal injury, business disputes and insurance matters. He is a judge pro tem with the Fresno County Superior Court and has been appointed by the Tulare County Superior Court to the approved mediator panel. Mr. Renberg is admitted to practice law in all courts in the State of California, including the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeal, and the U.S. District Courts in the Eastern, Northern, Southern and Central District.

In addition, he is a frequent seminar speaker on trial tactics, discovery and litigation. Topics that he has spoken on include:

  • Emerging Issues in California Indoor Air Quality and Toxic Mold Litigation
  • Advanced Trial Advocacy in California
  • Getting Evidence And Expert Testimony Admitted Into Court
  • Protecting Yourself From Malpractice
  • Dispute resolution and mediation strategies
  • Personal Injury Cases: Calculating and Proving Damages
  • Personal Injury Trials: Getting The Most Out of your Evidence
  • Effective Case Presentation at Trial
  • Trial Preparation From Start to Finish for Paralegals
